These are my papers on the evolution of cognition, by subject:

Cognition

Those with a wider scope are here. They are philosophical and concerned with the foundations of cognitive science.

On Turing Completeness, or Why We Are So Many

My main thesis is that Turing completeness is what characterizes our human cognition, and this is the paper where this is directly addressed, so it is a good place to start reading.

Link to the page of my paper on Turing completeness in figshare, and direct link to the pdf file. An older version is in SSRN.

The Intention of Intention

Taking "Representation and Reality" by Putnam as the best critique on the foundations of cognitive science, I propose evolutionary subjectivism modeled as problem solving by computing as the basis for cognitive science.

Link to the page of my paper on the philosophical foundations of cognitive science in figshare, and direct link to the pdf file.

Putnam's Rocks Are Clocks

Contains my analysis of Putnam's realization theorem in "Representation and Reality". It was a forerunner for the previous one.

Link to the page of my analysis of Putnam's realization theorem in figshare, and direct link to the pdf file.

Mathematics

The mathematical papers on the evolution of cognition are here.

Proof of Church's Thesis

It is shown that if we are Turing complete, then Church's thesis is true. And we are Turing complete, save for time or tape limitations and slips.

Link to the page of my "proof" of the Church-Turing thesis in the arXiv, and direct link to the pdf file. A second version is in figshare, with its pdf file.

Problem Theory

It presents a problem theory, which is an intuitionist set theory where the resolving subject is a computing device.

Link to the page of my problem theory in the arXiv, and direct link to the pdf file. A second version is in figshare, with its pdf file. And there is another version in The Internet Archive.

Linguistics

Though not myself a linguist, the evolution of cognition has something to say on linguistics.

Syntax Evolution: Problems and Recursion

We are syntactically too competent for syntax, because we are Turing complete and syntax does not require as much. This is an evolutionary anomaly, which we call the anomaly of syntax, that suggests us that syntax and problem solvig co-evolved in humans towars Turing completeness.

Link to the page of my paper on the co-evolution of syntax and problem solving in the arXiv, and direct link to the pdf file. A second version is in figshare, with its pdf file. Another version is in The Internet Archive, with its pdf file. There is an older and obsolete version titled "Resolution Machinery" in Cogprints, with its pdf file.

Universal Grammar is a universal grammar

It shows that "the human brain circuitry that implements language is Turing complete", it resolves some objections about it, and it extracts some implications on the architecture of human languages.

Link to my paper on the thesis that "the human brain circuitry that implements language is Turing complete", in figshare, with its pdf file. An older version is in SSRN. Another version is in The Internet Archive, with its pdf file. Another older and obsolete version is in Cogprints, with its pdf file.

Merge Is Not Recursion

My review of "Why Only Us" by Berwick and Chomsky. I have found two issues: their definition of recursion is not the mathematical one, and the strong minimalist thesis is too strong, and then it is not enough to explain the evolution of Merge to explain the evolution of language, which is what they are proposing in the book.

Link to the page of my review of "Why Only Us" by Berwick and Chomsky in figshare, and direct link to the pdf file. Another version is in The Internet Archive, with its pdf file.

On "On Recursion"

My review of "On Recursion" by Watumull et al. They modify the mathematical definition of "recursive function" to discriminate human language from other animal communication systems, but I argue instead that linguistic recursion should not deviate from mathematical recursion. It was a forerunner for "A Complete Hierarchy of Languages".

Link to the page of my review of "On Recursion" by Watumull et al. in figshare, and direct link to the pdf file.

A Complete Hierarchy of Languages

The thesis is that human language is best characterized as a Turing complete language. A hiearachy of languages is presented where languages are ordered by their expressiveness.

Link to the page of my paper on the complete hierarchy of languages in figshare, and direct link to the pdf file.
